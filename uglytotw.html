Preface

Back to Top

People often throw out items without giving a second thought. When looking at what some people consider “waste” can be really interesting to look or analyze over. For example, looking at a discarded packaging tells you what items they may enjoy or maybe even their hobbies. With this idea, I decided to record and share my trash to allow myself to take some time to analyze the things I discard without thinking about it. I believe that by recording these wastes, I will begin to realize things about myself that I did not know or thought deeply about. And I believe sharing this with others can allow me to know if my theory is correct that you can tell a lot about someone’s character from their trash.

Expanding on this concept, the act of recording discarded items can serve as a mirror reflecting various facets of one's lifestyle, preferences, and even values. Each piece of trash tells a story. This could be a broken gadget that signifies a hobby or interest, packaging that hints at dietary choices or favorite brands, or everyday items that reveal routines and habits. By categorizing and analyzing these items, patterns may emerge that highlight underlying behaviors or shifts in personal priorities over time. This allows us to get a deeper understanding of ourselves and I believe that is important for everyone.

Overview

Back to Top

Over the time of collecting a daily piece of trash, I have begun to notice how much I go through my daily items. Items such as my empty face wash tube or my conditioner bottle have made me realize I use these products quite often and repurchase them without a thought. With this idea, it has made me realize where my money is being placed without me thinking about it. I had always had the belief that I did not purchase a lot of leisure items, but seeing how fast I have gone through these products, tells me that I need to be more careful with how I use them. Another pattern I have noticed is how often I go through instant ramen. I have always had an idea of how much I consume the item, but seeing not only 1 but multiple packs go into the waste, has made me grow concerned at the amount I have been consuming. I have also taken notice of my friend’s supply, realizing mine disappearing much quicker than anyone I knew. This tells me I must be careful with how much I consume and must stop being lazy in cooking.

This project has so far been proving my theory correct, that it would tell me a lot about myself that I didn't give a second thought. However not only did it tell me things about myself, it brought back memories of the object. Whether it was something said about it in the past, or was used with a loved one a few days ago, it made me realize how something that people consider waste, holds a lot of memories. Based on everything I have learned and reminisced about, I have separated my trash of the week into 3 groups.

Self care

The first group I have is self care items. This includes self care items such as an elf primer packaging, toner pads, an empty conditioner bottle and a face wash tube. Most of these items I had obtained by ordering online on amazon, however I had gotten the conditioner bottle from a store in nyc.

Primer

Starting with the elf primer packaging, I had obtained this when realizing I had run out of my previous primer. It is a torn apart box due to me opening it roughly. I had gotten this new product because I had talked with some friends about products we could recommend to each other and one of them recommended this specific product. It was a pleasant talk with my female friends considering we often only talk about video games or shows

Conditioner

The empty conditioner bottle is a big red rectangular container. A little dried piece of the conditioner hangs from the spout, showing the consistent usage of the product. I had gotten this product while out with friends in nyc, memories of walking around talking about what i heard about this brand was very fun. I enjoyed the product so much, but unfortunately I used it all.

Face Wash

Similar to the conditioner bottle, I have an empty face wash tube. I have been using this product many times. I had come across it on social media, hearing it was affordable and worked well with dry skin. Hearing the great reviews, I had purchased it online and have been enjoying it (a little too much) since then.

Toner Pads

Finally the last item in the group is some used toner pads. They are thin rounded squares soaked. I also had heard great things about this brand and product and wanted to give them a try. One fond memory I have is when I had placed them on my face and walked over to my friend, who said I looked strange and scary. I then proceeded to put some on their faces as well. Unfortunately the item is not reusable so I must discard them after use.

Shared with loved ones

My second group is objects I have shared with loved ones. This includes an empty instant ramen package and an empty water bottle.

Instant ramen packet

The empty ramen package is a black thin plastic with red flames to demonstrate the spiciness of the product. I had gotten this item due to seeing my sister consuming the same brand. At a young age, I liked to try the same things my sister did. With this mentality, I decided to try this brand. I have many fond memories with this item. For example, the first time I tasted it, I nearly cried to my sister with how spicy it was while she laughed at me. Now I purchase this item online or in a supermarket. Another are the numerous times I had eaten instant ramen with my significant other late at night. As college students with no kitchen, we resorted to instant ramen to fill our midnight cravings. It was always a fond time eating this while watching our favorite show.

Water Bottle

Following along with this, my second item being an empty water bottle. It is a clear plastic bottle with some leftover water droplets inside. I retrieved this item from chugging it down after eating the spicy instant noodles. My significant other would constantly be stealing my water bottle and finishing them himself. We would jokingly argue on who steals more water than the other and would have to resort to buying a large amount of cases of water.

Hoarding

My third group is items I had thought would come in handy but really didnt. This includes a black face mask, a blue ribbon, and a paper with written problems on it.

Ribbon

My next item is a blue ribbon I had gotten as part of a gift I was given. It is a thin baby blue ribbon with a triangular cut at the end. I believed the ribbon was a pretty shade of blue and was fond of ribbons in hair. I figured one day I could use it to tie my hair one day but never did so. I remember trying to tie my hair with it but failing miserably and getting frustrated with it. I had kept it in my desk for a long time and decided to finally get rid of it.

Face mask

The black mask is soft and slightly creased from me folding it in my bag. I had gotten this from a pack my mother gave to me before college. My nose often gets very red when it is cold out and I would sometimes try to use a mask to help prevent that from happening. However I often forget or feel awkward wearing it so it unfortunately ends up in my bag for a long period of time. It reminds me of the time of the covid pandemic when everybody would wear the mask and it was considered normal. I used to be insecure about my face so I actually was fond of the mask idea. I decided to discard this item since it had been sitting in my bag for too long.

Scrap paper

Lastly I have a white scrap paper I used to write some practice problems on. I had used it while studying for one of my classes and figured I could possibly need it one day in the future. or even if one of my other friends would need it. Unfortunately I realized I would never look back on the sheet of people and decided it’s time to get rid of it. I remember the work was for my physics class and I had a tough time studying and understanding the material. The work done on this paper was the only material I felt confident in.

Item of the Week

The item of this week is a piece of scrap paper I had written old practice physics problems on! I have decided to choose this item due to the fact I have been struggling with physics 2 recently. Looking back at the scrap paper reminds me of the nights I spent up late trying to understand the material with my friends.

Physics had always been a subject I struggled in and unfortunately had to take it to continue my bachelors of science in computer science (I did not want to take chemistry). Days, Weeks, Months I had spent not understanding the topics at all and was going into a mental spiral. Thankfully I had my friends help me through. They had kept my spirits high and continued to help me in understanding the concepts even when they were struggling as well. This is something I will always remember and be thankful for. Considering I had to take physics 2 I figured keeping these papers would do me some good, but sadly they were put in the pile of items I hoard.

Scrap paper and practice problems go back to when paper was first invented in ancient China around the 2nd century BCE. In the past, paper was made from items such as old rags and bark, and leftover sheets were used for quick notes or rough sketches, what we now call “scrap paper.” As papermaking spread and more people learned to read and write, teachers began creating simple practice questions to help students master new skills. Today, we still use scrap paper and practice problems for everything from math drills to brainstorming ideas, proving that sometimes the simplest tools can have a lasting impact on learning.
